Hinagdanan Cave

Hinagdanan Cave, which translates as "laddered" in Cebuano, is a 100-meter-long cave located in Dauis, a coastal municipality in Bohol. According to locals, the cave was founded by accident by its landowner, who constructed a ladder to explore its interior after seeing an odd hole in his property. It was utilized as a hideaway by locals during World War II to escape the Japanese Imperial Army. Hinagdanan is a karst cave, which means it is made of limestone. It has multiple parallel stalactite and stalagmite formations as well as a freshwater lagoon perfect for taking a dip.

Location: Dauis, Bohol
