Tabon Cave

Tabon Caves, dubbed as the “cradle of civilization” of the Philippines, is a group of caves known for their anthropological and historical value. It is located on the western coastline of Quezon town in southern Palawan, one of the best islands in the Philippines. Tabon Caves is one of the few places in Southeast Asia where Homo Sapiens fossils have been discovered. Between 1962 and 1966, anthropologist Dr. Robert Fox and a team from the National Museum of the Philippines unearthed the Tabon Man, one of the Philippines' earliest known human skeleton remains, dating back to 14,000 B.C.

Location: Quezon town, Palawan
