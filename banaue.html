Banaue Rice Terraces, Ifugao

Constructed centuries ago by the ancestors of the Ifugao indigenous people, the Banaue Rice Terraces were designated as a National Cultural Treasure by the government in 1973. The culture of the Ifugao people, which was significantly influenced by agriculture and rice farming, is embodied in these terraces. The numerous agricultural ceremonies associated with rice production demonstrate the significance of these terraces. The extensive knowledge of the Ifugao people in earthwork, stonework, irrigation, and maintaining the integrity of the rice terraces is evident in the traditional and indigenous building techniques used to construct the terraces.

Location: Intramurals, Manila
